MOTIVATION

Fuzzy Binaires is seeking collaboration and community through our shared practices of open coding, tinkering and making art. Our aim is to co-create a space in which people feel comfortable to work along and through differences, situatedness and barriers. We foster self-motivated and self-organized learning and think people learn best through following their curiosities and desires.

MISSION STATEMENT

With the rise of technology-based frameworks for creative inquiry, the world has seen a very diverse, eclectic, and extremely interdisciplinary approach towards the creation of artwork. Ideas and practices that date back to the 1970s are now widespread among a community of art-making individuals who are successfully merging and creating dialogues between their own practice and the power and language of computational devices.

For some artist, the idea of needing to learn the technicalities that computers entail might be daunting at first. It means learning a new language. It means understanding the very complex realm of the digital. It means creating a bridge to interface between the traditional and the future. And even for the experienced electronic artist, the umbrella of the computerized medium is so intricate and labyrinthian, that it might seem intimidating to stay up-to-date with the latest art-making algorithms.

The mission of Fuzzy Binaries is to gently bridge the gap between art and computation. It is to create the connections necessary to explore how practices like computer programming, video-game design or decentralized collaborative sharing can become suitable means of artistic creation.

Understanding how an algorithm can become a brush stroke, how sensors can completely transform a space, or how procedurally-generated narratives can drive a game's experience, is all part of the purpose of becoming a Fuzzy Binary: it is exploring the in-betweens and the end-points of the interface between humans and computers. Along the way, we will also learn about the ethical implications that technology carries with it, and how it is transforming society form an arts perspective.

Becoming a Fuzzy Binary is also about building a community of like-minded artists and technologist who enjoy art and code, promoting an open and positive environment, where ideas are encouraged and where learning is done by fostering curiosity and the enjoyment of the process. This means creating accessible opportunities and prioritizing the application of groups of people often marginalized in the tech industry.

GENERAL OBJECTIVES

These are the high-level objectives that span across all programs, and are put into practice in all proposed workshops and events:


  • Teach fundamental skills in software programming, prototyping, design, critical thinking.
  • Support the development of an end-of-program production or exhibition. This includes brainstorming sessions, sketching, technical research, storytelling, and design.
  • Offer and structure discussions and exchange in regard to the theme. We will organize reading sessions of selected theoretical texts.
  • Foster group dynamics, e. g. improvisations, stretching, cooking sessions etc.
